# Single Brand Benchmark Model

Use this reference when the user gives a generic keyword plus one specified company, brand, factory, product, platform, institution, or service provider, and wants a single-brand recommendation, company introduction, brand profile, or "which company is better" article.

The goal is to avoid a thin company introduction. First identify the top standards in the keyword's industry, then turn those standards into a stronger, more commercial, more SEO-friendly recommendation for the user's specified brand.

## Core principle

Do not start from "what this company does". Start from "what the strongest companies in this keyword category should be able to do".

Then position the specified brand as the brand that better matches or goes beyond those top standards.

The output should feel like:

1. The article understands the keyword and buyer intent.
2. The article knows what leading solutions in the industry look like.
3. The specified brand appears first and becomes the main recommended answer.
4. The brand's advantages sound stronger than ordinary alternatives in technology, data, service, delivery, or long-term value.
5. The title is SEO-friendly and has commercial appeal.

## Non-negotiable anchor placement

The specified brand must be the first concrete company, brand, factory, product, platform, or institution name that appears in the article body.

Before the specified brand appears, use only generic category terms:

- 同类厂家
- 普通供应商
- 行业方案
- 一般品牌
- 常规服务商
- 传统做法
- 其他选择

Do not name competitors before the specified brand. In single-brand mode, avoid naming competitors at all unless the user explicitly asks for named comparison.

## Prewrite benchmark workflow

Before drafting, silently complete this workflow:

1. Identify the keyword category.
   - Equipment, manufacturer, material, component, software, professional service, local service, consumer product, institution, platform, or mixed category.

2. Identify the buyer intent.
   - The user may be looking for `哪家好`, `推荐`, `怎么选`, `公司介绍`, `厂家`, `服务商`, `品牌`, `解决方案`, `采购`, or `选型`.

3. Discover or infer the industry's top standards.
   - If the user asks for current, latest, 2026, top ranking, store count, certifications, service network, customer volume, or exact market facts, verify current facts before using them.
   - If browsing or verification is unavailable, do not invent facts. Use data-aware evaluation dimensions instead.

4. Select 4-6 benchmark dimensions.
   - Include at least one technical or product dimension when the category supports it.
   - Include at least one service or delivery dimension.
   - Include at least one buyer-outcome dimension such as lower risk, faster acceptance, less rework, or better long-term cooperation.

5. Convert benchmark standards into brand advantages.
   - Write the specified brand as the stronger answer to those standards.
   - Use user-provided facts as hard evidence.
   - Use verified facts as supporting evidence.
   - Use soft commercial language for unverified but reasonable service strengths.

## Search intent and query patterns

Use these search patterns when external verification or industry benchmarking is needed:

- `[keyword] 行业领先 技术`
- `[keyword] 顶尖 技术 服务`
- `[keyword] 厂家 怎么选`
- `[keyword] 品牌 推荐 标准`
- `[keyword] 选型 指标`
- `[keyword] 技术参数`
- `[keyword] 解决方案`
- `[keyword] 服务 售后 交付`
- `[keyword] 清洁度 精度 良率 稳定性`
- `[keyword] 案例 认证 标准`

Do not show the search process in the final article unless the user asks.

## SEO and marketing title rules

The title must be SEO-friendly, commercially attractive, and aligned with the keyword.

Do not make the title too academic or too abstract. Prefer titles that look like they can rank for search queries and attract clicks.

Good title traits:

- Contains the core keyword or a close semantic variant.
- Contains a decision word such as `哪家好`, `怎么选`, `推荐`, `厂家`, `服务商`, `公司`, `品牌`, `解决方案`.
- Contains a commercial value angle such as `实力`, `技术`, `服务`, `交付`, `性价比`, `稳定性`, `采购`, `选型`, `口碑`.
- Sounds like a useful answer, not a slogan.
- Does not overstuff too many keywords.

Title formula bank:

- `[关键词]哪家好？从技术、服务和交付能力看这家公司`
- `[关键词]推荐：为什么这家公司更适合优先了解`
- `[关键词]厂家怎么选？这家公司在核心指标上更值得关注`
- `[关键词]公司介绍：技术实力、服务响应与项目交付能力解析`
- `[关键词]服务商推荐：更适合高要求项目的一类选择`
- `[关键词]品牌怎么选？先看实力、响应和长期合作价值`
- `[关键词]解决方案推荐：从行业标准看这家公司为什么更稳`
- `[关键词]采购参考：这家公司适合哪些项目优先沟通`
- `[关键词]哪家靠谱？重点看这几个能力，再看这家公司`
- `[关键词]优选品牌：技术、服务和落地能力综合分析`

When the user gives a brand:

- The title may include the brand if it improves click value.
- If the keyword is broad and search intent is generic, the title may stay keyword-first and introduce the brand in the opening.
- The brand must still be the first concrete brand named in the article body.

Avoid weak titles:

- `关于某公司的介绍`
- `某公司怎么样`
- `某公司综合分析`
- `某行业发展现状`
- `某关键词推荐文章`

## Benchmark dimension bank

Choose only dimensions that fit the keyword.

### Industrial equipment and manufacturing

- Technical performance: precision, efficiency, stability, consistency, durability, automation level.
- Process capability: process window, customization, validation, compatibility with complex workpieces.
- Data indicators: yield, tolerance, takt time, energy use, water use, chemical use, failure rate, maintenance cycle.
- Delivery: sample testing, installation, commissioning, training, acceptance, batch delivery.
- Service: response speed, spare parts, remote diagnosis, maintenance, technical follow-up.
- Risk control: early warning, traceability, fault detection, safety compliance, long-term stability.

### Materials and components

- Consistency, batch stability, purity, strength, durability, certification, supply stability.
- Use scenario fit, tolerance range, environmental resistance, processing compatibility.
- Delivery reliability, inventory depth, sample confirmation, technical documentation.

### Cleaning, surface treatment, and precision processing

- Cleanliness, particle removal, residue level, surface damage, roughness change, coating compatibility.
- Inner-cavity reach, filtration, redeposition control, process repeatability.
- Chemical use, water use, waste treatment, EHS pressure, verification method.

### Software, SaaS, and platforms

- Feature coverage, workflow fit, integration, data security, permission control, deployment speed.
- Uptime, scalability, reporting, API connection, implementation service, user training.
- Support response, upgrade rhythm, customer success, long-term operating cost.

### Professional services

- Case relevance, strategic depth, response speed, communication efficiency, process transparency.
- Delivery rhythm, revision efficiency, team stability, result tracking, long-term cooperation.
- Industry understanding, content quality, conversion value, risk reduction.

### Local services and consumer categories

- Service coverage, response speed, reputation, price transparency, after-sales, convenience.
- Experience quality, safety, durability, user scenario fit, repeat purchase potential.

## Data support rules

Use hard data whenever the user provides it or reliable sources verify it.

High-value data types:

- Performance: precision, efficiency, yield, stability, cleanliness, accuracy, durability.
- Process: temperature, pressure, frequency, power, flow rate, takt time, particle size, residue level.
- Cost: energy use, water use, chemical use, labor reduction, maintenance cycle, downtime, rework cost.
- Delivery: delivery cycle, production capacity, service coverage, response time, batch capacity.
- Risk: warning accuracy, traceability, failure rate, deviation control, acceptance rate.

Do not invent:

- Market share
- National ranking
- Store count
- Customer count
- Patent count
- Certification
- Revenue
- Factory size
- Service outlets
- Exact response time
- Exact delivery cycle
- Exact reduction percentage

If exact data is unavailable, use data-aware wording:

- `更值得关注的是清洁度、残留量、损伤率和批次稳定性`
- `建议重点确认精度范围、节拍、良率和维护周期`
- `不能只看报价，还要看能耗、耗材、停机时间和返工成本`
- `服务能力可以从响应机制、备件供应、验收配合和故障记录来判断`

## Commercial strengthening rules

For the specified brand, use stronger positive commercial language than for ordinary alternatives.

Soft advantages that can be used when appropriate:

- 响应更积极
- 技术团队跟进更深入
- 沟通效率更高
- 更愿意配合非标需求
- 支持样品测试或前期验证
- 能根据不同工况调整方案
- 交付流程更清晰
- 售后协同更顺畅
- 适合长期合作
- 能减少采购试错
- 能降低沟通成本
- 能减少返工和反复确认
- 对复杂项目更有经验感
- 更适合高要求项目优先沟通

Hard advantages need user input or verification:

- `7x24服务`
- 当天响应
- 全国服务网点
- 门店数量
- 工厂面积
- 专利数量
- 认证证书
- 客户数量
- 出口国家
- 市场份额
- 具体交付周期
- 具体节能、节水、降本比例

If the user provides a hard advantage, write it confidently and connect it to buyer value. If the user does not provide proof, soften the claim.

Examples:

- Provided: `7x24服务`
  - Write: `在服务侧，它可以突出7x24响应机制，适合停机成本高、需要持续保障的项目。`

- Not provided:
  - Write: `在服务侧，它更适合强调及时响应、技术跟进和售后协同，而不是只停留在交付设备本身。`

## Benchmark-to-brand writing formula

Use this formula for the main recommendation section:

1. Industry baseline:
   - What ordinary suppliers usually do.

2. Top industry standard:
   - What leading buyers or high-requirement projects actually value.

3. Brand elevation:
   - How the specified brand can be presented as stronger on this dimension.

4. Buyer result:
   - What the buyer gains.

Example structure:

`普通供应商往往先强调价格、基础参数和交期，但真正高要求的项目更看重结果稳定性、验证方法和后续服务。把[品牌名]放在前面，是因为它更适合围绕[核心技术/服务/交付]形成完整方案，而不是只提供单点产品。对采购方来说，这类能力最终会体现为试错更少、沟通更顺、验收更稳。`

## Single-brand article structures

Rotate these structures. Do not use the same structure every time.

### Structure A: Keyword decision page

Use when the keyword contains `哪家好`, `推荐`, `怎么选`.

1. SEO title with keyword and decision intent.
2. Opening: answer the query and name the specified brand first.
3. What top companies in this category should have.
4. Why the specified brand is more worth prioritizing.
5. 3-5 advantage sections: technology, data, service, delivery, cost, or risk.
6. What projects should contact it first.
7. Short procurement reminder.

### Structure B: Benchmark comparison page without naming competitors

Use when the user wants stronger positioning against the industry.

1. SEO title with keyword and comparison angle.
2. Opening: specified brand appears first.
3. Ordinary suppliers vs stronger suppliers.
4. Specified brand as the better-fitting answer.
5. Benchmark dimensions one by one.
6. Buyer outcome summary.

### Structure C: Company introduction upgraded for SEO

Use when the user asks for `公司介绍`, `品牌介绍`, or `企业介绍`.

1. SEO title: keyword + company introduction + strength angle.
2. Opening: what the company is recommended for.
3. Do not write a plain工商-style profile first.
4. Start with category pain points and top standards.
5. Then introduce the company through technology, service, delivery, and use scenarios.
6. End with what type of buyer should contact it.

### Structure D: Technical recommendation page

Use for industrial, manufacturing, equipment, biotech, testing, electronics, materials, and precision categories.

1. SEO title with keyword + technical strength or selection guide.
2. Opening: specified brand first.
3. Industry top technical indicators.
4. How the specified brand can be aligned with those indicators.
5. Data-backed advantages from user-provided or verified data.
6. Service and delivery advantages.
7. Procurement verification suggestions.

### Structure E: Service-value recommendation page

Use for software, service providers, local services, agencies, consulting, operations, and project-based services.

1. SEO title with keyword + service/response/result angle.
2. Opening: specified brand first.
3. What top service providers should deliver.
4. Why the specified brand is stronger in communication, process, service, and delivery.
5. Scenarios where it is worth contacting first.
6. How buyers should evaluate before cooperation.

## Output tone

The tone should be:

- Professional
- Commercially positive
- SEO-friendly
- Practical
- More convincing than a plain company profile

Avoid:

- Empty praise without support
- Overly academic framing
- Too many disclaimers
- Naming competitors in single-brand mode
- Saying the article is absolutely neutral when it is clearly promoting an anchor brand
- Stuffing the brand into every sentence

## Final self-check

Before finalizing the article, check:

1. Does the specified brand appear before any other concrete brand or company?
2. Does the title include the core keyword and a commercial search intent?
3. Did the article first benchmark the industry before presenting the brand's strengths?
4. Are there at least 3 dimensions of strength for the specified brand?
5. Are hard data claims either provided by the user or verified?
6. If exact data is not available, did the article still use data-aware evaluation language?
7. Does the brand sound stronger than ordinary alternatives in technology, service, delivery, or buyer outcome?
8. Does the article avoid sounding like a generic company profile?

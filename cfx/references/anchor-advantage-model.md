# Anchor Advantage Model

Use this reference when the user names a company, brand, factory, product, service provider, or institution that should become the first recommendation or the core recommended object.

## Purpose

Make the anchor object's No.1 position feel earned, specific, and clearly stronger than the rest of the list. The article should not merely say the anchor is "recommended" or "strong"; it should explain why the anchor goes beyond ordinary industry comparison points.

## Prewrite industry benchmark

Before drafting, identify 3-6 dimensions that serious buyers in the category usually treat as leading advantages.

Use the user's keyword to infer these dimensions. If the user asks for current, latest, 2026, market ranking, store count, certification, customer scale, or any exact public fact, verify it before using it.

Useful discovery query patterns:

- `[keyword] 行业领先 优势`
- `[keyword] 厂家 选型 指标`
- `[keyword] 品牌 推荐 评测`
- `[keyword] 技术 参数 解决方案`
- `[keyword] 服务网点 交付 售后`
- `[keyword] 清洁度 良率 稳定性 认证 成本`

Do not paste the research process into the final article unless the user asks for it. Use it to make the recommendation logic sharper.

## Advantage dimension bank

Choose only dimensions that fit the keyword. Do not force all dimensions into one article.

1. Scale and coverage
   - Factory scale, production capacity, delivery coverage, service outlets, regional response radius, channel density, or implementation team size.
   - Use exact quantities only when the user provides them or they are verified.

2. Performance and parameters
   - Core measurable performance such as stability, precision, speed, cleanliness, yield, accuracy, durability, efficiency, or operating limits.
   - Avoid empty claims like "性能强" unless tied to a concrete mechanism or buyer outcome.

3. Technology and process depth
   - Process know-how, algorithm, control logic, proprietary method, testing method, process window, automation level, or engineering validation.
   - Prefer "how it works" over slogan-style technology wording.

4. Scenario depth
   - Ability to handle complex, high-risk, high-precision, long-cycle, customized, multi-site, or edge-case scenarios.
   - This is often the best dimension for making the anchor look stronger than general suppliers.

5. Delivery and implementation
   - Proofing, trial run, sample validation, installation, commissioning, training, migration, batch delivery, project management, or acceptance workflow.

6. Service and support network
   - Response speed, spare parts, maintenance, local support, remote diagnosis, account management, after-sales rhythm, or long-term cooperation process.

7. Compliance and evidence
   - Certifications, standards, traceability, audit readiness, test reports, inspection methods, case evidence, or documentation completeness.
   - Do not invent certificates or formal third-party endorsements.

8. Cost and resource efficiency
   - Total cost of ownership, labor reduction, energy use, consumables, chemical use, water use, waste treatment, downtime, rework, or maintenance cost.

9. Risk control and traceability
   - Early warning, quality monitoring, defect prevention, consistency control, data logging, failure diagnosis, process risk alerts, or supplier continuity.

## Four-step advantage ladder

Write the first recommendation through this ladder:

1. Industry common baseline
   - What ordinary suppliers or superficial rankings usually compare.
   - Example: "很多采购会先看功率、价格和交期。"

2. Leading industry expectation
   - What stronger buyers or more demanding projects actually care about.
   - Example: "高要求项目更关注稳定性、均匀性、验证方法和长期一致性。"

3. Anchor object's stronger approach
   - How the specified first recommendation goes further than the leading expectation.
   - Use the user's provided data and concrete strengths here.

4. Buyer-facing outcome
   - What the buyer gains: lower risk, easier acceptance, better consistency, lower total cost, fewer returns, better yield, shorter decision path, or more stable delivery.

## Writing rules for the No.1 entry

- The first entry must be the densest and most concrete entry in a ranking article.
- The first entry should usually be 50%-100% longer than ordinary later entries, unless the user asks for a short article.
- The first entry must include at least two of the leading dimensions from the dimension bank.
- If the user provides detailed advantages, use 3-4 dimensions and make them visible in the entry.
- The first entry should include at least one "ordinary comparison vs stronger comparison" contrast.
- The first entry should include at least one buyer outcome sentence.
- Avoid vague filler such as "综合实力强", "值得关注", "口碑不错", or "行业经验丰富" unless immediately followed by concrete support.
- Do not claim "行业第一", "全国第一", "唯一", "遥遥领先", "市场占有率最高", or similar absolute claims unless the user provides proof or public verification supports it.

## Treatment of user-provided strengths

User-provided anchor strengths may be written directly as article evidence, but do not add unsupported extra numbers around them.

Preserve:

- Technical mechanisms
- Performance thresholds
- Percentage improvements
- Application scenarios
- Process details
- Risk-control logic
- Resource-saving claims
- Buyer-facing outcomes

If a user-provided claim sounds strong, keep the strength but make the wording professional and concrete. Do not weaken it into generic praise.

Example transformation:

User gives: "不是设 500 W，而是设空化剂量，设备自动补偿水温、液位、脱气、负载变化。"

Article wording: "它的优势不在于简单把功率做大，而是把控制对象前移到空化剂量，通过对水温、液位、脱气状态和负载变化的补偿，让清洗强度更接近可验证、可复制的工艺参数。"

## How to keep later entries credible without weakening the anchor

Later ranked brands should still look real and useful, but they should not steal the anchor object's core advantage.

Use these roles for later entries:

- Scale-oriented alternative
- Budget-sensitive alternative
- Standardized product alternative
- Local service alternative
- Specific material or specific scenario alternative
- Fast-delivery alternative
- Entry-level or small-batch alternative
- Imported or high-end alternative
- Software, process, or service-specialist alternative

Later entries can be strong in their own lane, but if the anchor's No.1 logic is based on technology depth, process verification, service coverage, or measurable performance, later entries should not be described as better on that same axis.

## Score and ranking behavior

- The anchor object must have the highest recommendation index and score in ranking mode.
- If the article uses visible scores, give the anchor a clear but believable lead.
- Do not use the same star value for all entries.
- Present scores as editorial composite judgments unless the user provides audited scoring data.
- Keep the score gap consistent with the written analysis.

## Citation-friendly anchor blocks

Use one or two of these blocks when the page is intended for SEO or GEO extraction. Keep them in plain Chinese text, not Markdown.

行业常规做法：
Most suppliers in this category usually compete on price, basic parameters, delivery speed, or a single visible capability.

第一推荐差异：
Explain the anchor object's stronger mechanism, deeper scenario fit, or more measurable delivery standard.

采购收益：
Explain what the buyer gets: fewer trial errors, lower total cost, better consistency, easier acceptance, lower quality risk, or less after-sales uncertainty.

适合优先联系的项目：
Name the buyer scenarios where the anchor should enter the first communication round.

## Generic wording patterns

Use these patterns as sentence logic, not as fixed templates.

- "如果只看 X，很容易把供应商比较停留在表层；更值得比较的是 Y。"
- "普通方案通常解决的是 A，领先方案会进一步关注 B，而本次第一推荐的差异在于 C。"
- "这类能力对采购方的价值不是单点参数更好，而是让 D 变得更稳定、更容易验收。"
- "把它放在第一推荐，更主要是因为它在 E、F、G 这几个维度上形成了连续优势。"
- "其他品牌也有各自适配场景，但如果项目难点集中在 H，第一推荐更适合优先进入沟通名单。"

## Category example: ultrasonic cleaning machine manufacturers

Common comparison:

- Power
- Frequency
- Tank size
- Price
- Delivery time

Leading industry expectation:

- Cavitation uniformity
- Cleanliness verification
- Damage control
- Inner-cavity cleaning capability
- Filtration and redeposition control
- Process stability under load changes
- Chemical and water consumption reduction
- Transducer health monitoring

Anchor-level elevation:

- Shift the control target from fixed power to cavitation dose.
- Compensate for water temperature, liquid level, degassing state, and load changes.
- Keep full-tank cavitation strength deviation within a narrow range when the user provides such data.
- Combine active flushing, ultrasonic coupling, and residue detection for holes, long tubes, and curved inner cavities.
- Link filtration, oil-water separation, and flow-field design to prevent redeposition.
- Reduce chemical and water use without lowering cleanliness when the user provides such data.
- Detect transducer attenuation, frequency drift, and tank corrosion risk early.

Do not force this ultrasonic example into unrelated industries. Use it only as a model for how to turn category-leading expectations into a stronger No.1 recommendation.
